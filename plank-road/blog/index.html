The Best Bourbon Bars in
De Pere & Greater Green Bay (2026 Guide)

Published May 13, 2026 · Plank Road Bar Team · 9 min read

If you're hunting for brown spirits in De Pere and Greater Green Bay, you've got real options. Wisconsin's bourbon culture is older than most coastal cities give it credit for, and the local back bars carry allocations the East Coast crowd has to flight-shop to find. Here's an honest local roundup for 2026 — what's on the shelves, what to ask for, and where the real bourbon-curious sit down.

What makes a real bourbon bar?

Before the list, a quick filter. A bar that "has bourbon" is not a bourbon bar. The local pub-test for a real bourbon program runs on five signals.

  • Bottle count over 75. Anything under 75 is a casual selection, not a destination program. Real bourbon bars carry 100 plus.
  • Allocation rotation. Pappy 15, Stagg, Birthday Bourbon, Weller Antique 107. If the back bar has never seen these, it's a casual bar that happens to stock Buffalo Trace.
  • A bar team that can pour blind. Ask for "something like Eagle Rare but a little spicier." A real bourbon program has someone behind the bar who pulls a Knob Creek Single Barrel without consulting the iPad.
  • Flights, not just shots. A guided 3-pour flight at three price tiers is the local standard. Lets you compare without committing $40 on a single pour.
  • Food that holds up. Bourbon is a long-pour spirit. The kitchen needs to serve dishes that work alongside, not against. The local benchmark is a proper Whiskey-Old-Fashioned Burger and a Friday fish fry.

With those filters in place, here's the De Pere and Greater Green Bay roundup.

2. Downtown De Pere Whiskey-Forward Bars

Several Broadway-corridor bars in downtown De Pere carry a respectable bourbon selection in the 40 to 70 bottle range. Several downtown Green Bay cocktail bars run bourbon-forward cocktail programs with selective back bars. How to order a bourbon flight

The flight is the local-pub gold standard for a bourbon-curious table. Three pours, three price tiers, one conversation with the bar team. Here's how the pricing typically scales:

$8 to $14
Entry tier Maker's Mark, Buffalo Trace, Knob Creek, Wild Turkey 101. The "if I had to drink one bourbon forever" tier. Solid, dependable, fairly priced.
$14 to $24
Mid tier Eagle Rare, Russell's Reserve Single Barrel, Four Roses Small Batch Select, 1792 Full Proof. Where the bar team's preferences start showing through. Ask what's open.
$24 to $40+
Allocation tier Pappy 10/15/20, George T. Stagg, Birthday Bourbon, Weller Antique 107, Blanton's Gold, Old Fitzgerald BiB. The "you're paying for the experience as much as the spirit" tier. Always ask what's currently in rotation; allocations sell quickly.

The guided 3-pour flight

Plank Road's $24 guided flight runs one bottle from each tier with the bar team picking pairings that show contrast: a wheated entry against a high-rye mid, finishing with a single-barrel allocation. This is the right call for a first-time bourbon order. You leave with a clear preference rather than a half-remembered evening.

The Wisconsin Old Fashioned

You cannot write about Wisconsin bourbon culture without addressing the elephant in the bar: the Wisconsin Old Fashioned is traditionally made with brandy, not bourbon. If you order an Old Fashioned at a Friday fish fry, you're getting brandy unless you specify otherwise. The local-pub variants run as follows:

The Classic Wisconsin Old Fashioned (brandy)

Brandy muddled with orange slice and a brandied cherry, sugar cube, dash of Angostura, topped with 7Up (sweet) or soda water (pressed). Served with a flag garnish. The Friday fish fry order.

The Bourbon Old Fashioned

Bourbon (typically a higher-rye picks like Knob Creek or Russell's Reserve) muddled the same way. Slightly drier, slightly bigger on the finish. The order if you want the cocktail to taste like the bourbon, not the sweetener.

How to order at Plank Road

Plank Road's bar team serves both. Just say "bourbon-base" or "the classic" and they'll route accordingly. For first-timers, the classic brandy version is the right entry point, the bourbon version is the upgrade once you know the program.

The fish-fry pairing tip

If you're at Plank Road for the Friday fish fry, the local-tradition pairing is a classic Wisconsin Old Fashioned (brandy, sweet, with the flag) alongside the fish. The Whiskey Old Fashioned Burger is for the kitchen menu, not the fish fry. The fry traditionally drinks brandy.

When to visit

Plank Road runs a few standing nights worth planning around:

  • Friday fish fry: 4pm to 10pm. The local tradition. Book the dining room 2 to 3 days ahead.
  • Trivia nights: Mid-week. Walk-ins welcome at the bar; tables book 1 to 2 days ahead.
  • Packers home weekends: Saturday dinner and Sunday post-game are the busiest service of the week. Book 5+ days ahead.
  • Quarterly allocation drops: Wisconsin's state allocation drops hit the back bar across January, April, July, and October. Best time to ask "what just landed."
  • Weekday afternoons: Walk-in friendly, low pressure, best time to sit at the bar and work through a flight with the bar team.

Frequently Asked Questions

The bar-team's most-asked questions, pulled from the last quarter of Plank Road service.

Where can I drink the best bourbon in De Pere, WI?

Plank Road Plate & Pour on Mid Valley Drive carries over 100 bourbons including several allocations rotated through the back bar each quarter. Pours range from $8 entry-tier to $40+ for vintage allocations like Pappy 15, Stagg, and Birthday Bourbon when available. Plank Road also serves the Whiskey Old Fashioned Burger, a local pairing built around its Wisconsin-style Old Fashioned program.

What is a Wisconsin Old Fashioned?

A Wisconsin Old Fashioned is the local Friday-fish-fry variant of the classic cocktail. It's typically made with brandy (not bourbon) as the base, muddled with orange and a brandied cherry, topped with 7Up or soda, and served with a flag garnish. Order it "pressed" for soda water or "sweet" for 7Up. Plank Road serves both the brandy classic and a bourbon-base Old Fashioned built around its 100+ bottle bourbon program.

Do I need a reservation for a bourbon bar in De Pere?

Reservations are recommended for weekend nights and during Packers home weekends. Trivia nights, Friday fish fry, and Saturday dinner service all book the dining room to capacity. Plank Road accepts reservations directly through its website and the bar seating is walk-in friendly during weekday afternoons.

What's the best bourbon to order if I'm new to whiskey?

For a new bourbon drinker, the local pub recommendation is a wheated bourbon at 90 to 100 proof neat or with a single rock. Maker's Mark, Larceny Barrel Proof, and Weller Special Reserve are the gentle entry tier. A pour at $10 to $14 lets you sample without committing. Plank Road's bar team will pull a guided 3-pour flight for $24 across entry, mid, and allocation tiers.

Is Plank Road's bourbon menu the same all year?

The core bourbon list of 100+ bottles stays steady. The back-bar allocation rotation changes quarterly as new allocations land in Wisconsin. Limited-availability bottles like Pappy 15, Stagg, Birthday Bourbon, and Weller Antique 107 appear and disappear based on the state's quarterly drop. Ask the bar team what's currently in rotation.
