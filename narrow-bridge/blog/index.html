Live Music Restaurants in Howard, WI:
The 2026 Guide for Where to Eat + Catch a Show

Published May 13, 2026 · Narrow Bridge House Team · 9 min read

Howard and the Green Bay suburbs have a quietly serious live music scene, you just have to know where to look. The standing-Friday-Saturday stages live in the restaurants that decided to build a real room for it, not the bars that occasionally book a DJ. Here's the 2026 local breakdown: where to sit down, what to order, and whose stage actually books a real act.

What separates a live music restaurant from a bar with a band?

Before the list, a quick filter. Plenty of bars host an acoustic act on a Friday. That's not a live music restaurant. A real live music dining destination shares five signals.

  • A real stage, not a corner. A built room with a studio-quality stage means the sound carries, the band actually wants to play there, and your dinner conversation isn't competing with the singer's monitor.
  • A standing weekend booking. Live music every Friday and Saturday means it's part of the program, not a marketing experiment.
  • Patio season programming. May through September is the local live-music high season. A real live-music restaurant runs the outdoor stage when the weather works.
  • Kitchen open until the music ends. Late-night dining is the local tell. A live music restaurant keeps the kitchen open until 11pm minimum on show nights.
  • Real Wisconsin food. Wood-fired pizza, hand-breaded fish, brick-build burgers, cheese curds, a real Friday fish fry. Not bar food. Pub food cooked properly.

With those filters in place, here's the actual Howard and Greater Green Bay roundup.

2. Greater Green Bay Restaurants with Occasional Music

Suamico + Ashwaubenon + downtown Green Bay · Schedule varies

Several Greater Green Bay restaurants book music on Friday and Saturday nights, often on a rotating-quarterly basis. The big difference vs Narrow Bridge: their bookings aren't standing-weekly, and the rooms are typically dining-first with the stage added on. Strong if you happen to catch a night they've booked, less reliable for "I want music tonight, where do I go" planning.

3. Downtown Green Bay Venue-Restaurants

Downtown Green Bay · Concert-night programming

A handful of downtown Green Bay venues serve dinner alongside ticketed concert nights. These are more venue-with-food than restaurant-with-stage. The food is a means to the show. Strong if you've already bought a ticket for a specific touring act, less suited to "I want to wander in for a Friday dinner with a band on."

What to order on a music night

The right order paces correctly through a 90-minute live set. Two share-board orders, two entrees, the table reaches the end of the set with everyone happy.

Wood-Fired Pizza ($16 to $22)

Shareable, paces well, the kitchen sends them out quickly during a busy show night. The local-favorite is the Lake Perch Pizza, a Narrow Bridge original: hand-breaded lake perch, lemon cream, capers, dill, wood-fired crust. The bar staff will pour a glass of dry Wisconsin Riesling alongside without being asked.

Hand-Breaded Lake Perch ($21)

The Friday-fish-fry tradition served Wisconsin-style. Three to four hand-breaded fillets, fries, slaw, tartar. Best ordered with a Wisconsin Old Fashioned (bourbon-base or brandy-base, your call). Heavy enough to slow you down through the second set.

Build-Your-Own Burger ($14 to $19)

The local burger destination in Howard for the under-30 crowd, half-pound brick-built, cheese choice, three toppings free, sides included. The kitchen routes these quickly during late-night service.

Cheese Curds for the Table

The Wisconsin-test. Hand-breaded, fried in small batches, never bagged. The order that says "we're staying for the second set."

The pacing tip

If you're sitting down for a 7pm reservation with a 9pm set start, order the share-board first, the entrees at the start of the set. The kitchen times this naturally during music nights. Your entrees land in time to eat through the first chorus, the shares carry you to the bridge.

When to book

The patterns that govern Narrow Bridge weekend reservations:

  • Friday + Saturday nights: book 2 to 4 days ahead in patio season (May to September). 1 to 2 days in shoulder season.
  • Packers home Saturdays: book 5 to 7 days ahead. The Saturday before a home game is the busiest dinner-and-music night of the week.
  • Patio: outdoor seating fills first. Mention the patio when you reserve if it's a priority.
  • Sunday acoustic sets: walk-in fine. Patio season only.
  • Touring-act nights: the calendar lists weekend acts 2 to 4 weeks ahead. If a specific act is announced, those nights fill 7 to 14 days in advance.
