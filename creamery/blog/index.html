Where to Get Bottomless Mimosas
in Green Bay (2026 Guide)

Published May 13, 2026 · The Creamery Editorial Team · 8 min read

If you're looking for bottomless mimosas in Green Bay, you have a handful of solid options downtown. The good ones share three things: real sparkling wine (not the dollar-bin stuff), a generous flavor list, and food that holds up to a 90-minute pour. Here's the local breakdown for 2026, with a fair-shake comparison of where the brunch crowd actually goes.

What makes a good bottomless mimosa brunch?

Before the list, a quick local primer. Wisconsin's brunch culture is generous: you're rarely paying for the drink alone, you're paying for the full Sunday-on-the-deck experience. The deals that hold up locally tend to share five traits.

  • Real sparkling wine. Not Prosecco-labeled-but-actually-house-pour. Ask if you're not sure.
  • Genuine flavor variety. Three flavors isn't bottomless, it's a tasting flight. A real bottomless menu runs 8 to 20 flavors.
  • A reasonable time window. 90 minutes is the local standard. Shorter feels stingy. Longer is rare in Green Bay.
  • Food worth eating. The drink package is the lure; the brunch is what you remember. Eggs Benedict and brunch burgers are the local litmus tests.
  • An outdoor option in summer. Green Bay summers are short. If a spot has patio or deck seating, that's where you want to be from May through September.

With that out of the way, here's the actual list.

The Green Bay bottomless mimosa roundup

1. The Creamery Downtown

117 S Washington St · Downtown Green Bay · $24 / 90 min

Made-from-scratch brunch on the City Deck overlooking the Fox River. Seventeen flavors of bottomless mimosas, served alongside an eggs benedict flight, brunch burgers, and locally roasted coffee. The pour is real Champagne-method sparkling, not bargain-bin Prosecco. The flavor list rotates seasonally but always includes the local-favorite blood orange and the seasonal cherry-vanilla.

  • 17 rotating mimosa flavors (more than anywhere else in the city)
  • 90-minute window, reset on every reservation
  • City Deck seating from May through September
  • Eggs Benedict Flight: three signature variations on one plate ($19)
  • Top 10 Yelp Brunch in Green Bay, multiple years running

The Creamery is the call if you want the full Green Bay brunch experience: scratch food, real sparkling, City Deck views, and the highest flavor count in town. Reservations book out 3 to 5 days ahead in summer and on Packers home weekends.

2. Hagemeister Park

325 N Washington St · Downtown Green Bay · Mimosa specials weekend brunch

One of the most-known weekend brunch destinations in downtown Green Bay. Hagemeister runs mimosa specials on Saturday and Sunday brunch, with a tighter flavor list but a strong patio in the warm months. Better for a single-flavor pitcher experience than a flavored-mimosa flight.

3. Local Cocktail Bars (Rotating Specials)

Downtown Green Bay · Brunch hours weekends only

A handful of downtown bars run brunch programs on Saturday and Sunday with bottomless mimosa add-ons. These tend to skew bar-first, brunch-second, so the food is lighter and the drink program is the focus. Worth checking on weekends when you want a later-morning drink-led brunch instead of a food-led one.

4. Suburban Brunch Spots (De Pere, Howard, Ashwaubenon)

Greater Green Bay metro · Varies by location

If you're outside of downtown, both Plank Road Plate & Pour in De Pere and Narrow Bridge Plate & Pour in Howard serve weekend brunch with mimosa packages. Plank Road is the bourbon-bar-meets-brunch option for the more whiskey-curious crowd. Narrow Bridge runs brunch alongside its live-music programming.

What to order with your mimosa

The mimosa is the headline but the food is what makes the table feel like brunch. Three orders that pace correctly across a 90-minute window:

Eggs Benedict Flight ($19 at The Creamery)

Three signature benedicts on one plate. The classic Canadian-bacon variation, a smoked-salmon variation, and a seasonal third (currently mushroom-tarragon). Heavy enough to slow the pour without dragging the meal.

Brunch Burger ($16)

House-ground beef, fried egg, applewood bacon, smoked cheddar. The local pick when the table is split between brunch traditionalists and burger-for-breakfast types.

Shareable Sides

Skillet hash, breakfast potatoes, the house biscuit. Order one or two for the table. They're the gap-filler between rounds of mimosas.

A note on pacing

The 90-minute window resets at first pour, not at reservation time. Order food first, then drinks. Your server will time the window from when the first mimosa hits the table, which gives you the full hour-and-a-half of pours without losing time to waiting on the kitchen.

When to book

Weekend brunch in downtown Green Bay books out. The patterns to plan around:

  • Packers home weekends: book 7+ days ahead. The Saturday before a home game is the busiest brunch slot of the week.
  • City Deck season (May through September): outdoor seating fills first. Friday-for-Sunday is the realistic booking window.
  • Holidays: Mother's Day, Easter, Father's Day, and the weekend before Christmas are essentially fully booked 2 to 3 weeks out.
  • Off-peak (winter weekdays): walk-in is usually fine. Tuesday through Thursday brunch is the most relaxed sitting of the week.

The one-paragraph answer

The Creamery Downtown is the most generous and most-booked bottomless mimosa brunch in Green Bay: 17 flavors, 90 minutes, $24, City Deck seating in summer, scratch food, and the best benedict flight in town. Hagemeister Park is the strong runner-up downtown. If you're in the suburbs, Plank Road in De Pere and Narrow Bridge in Howard run weekend brunch with mimosa packages of their own. Book at least 3 days ahead from May through September and 7+ days ahead for Packers home weekends.
